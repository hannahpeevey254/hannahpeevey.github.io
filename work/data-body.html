01 — Concept
THE IDEA

A collective built from six AI personas, each trained on a different slice of my real, intimate data — location history, sleep cycles, biometric readings, messages, search history, calendar.

Together they form a society inside me. A digital ecosystem built of 6 AI personas using different slices of my real, intimate data to become a society inside me.

02 — System
HOW IT WORKS

Six local Ollama instances, each shaped by a specific data category. A multi-agent orchestration layer routes questions to all six simultaneously and surfaces their responses in real time.

They don't agree. They debate. They reflect the fragmented, contradictory self that data produces.

03 — Interface
WHAT YOU SEE

A web interface where visitors interact with the six AI personas — each colour-coded, each responding from its own data slice. Ask questions. Watch them debate. The disagreement between them is the point.

04 — Questions
WHAT IT ASKS

When strangers interact with the system, they reveal as much as the system does. The questions they ask become a mirror.

01 — Concept
THE MARKET

Your biometric data isn't personal. It's a product. Your biological statistics become microeconomic units the moment they enter your phone.

Market of Me makes that visible — a speculative data stock exchange where intimate body data is listed, traded, and valued on an open market.

02 — System
HOW IT WORKS

Built with JavaScript and Supabase, the platform collects confessional biometric data through an anonymous form — age, sleep, anxiety, screen time, weight, heart rate — and feeds it into a live stock market interface.

I betrayed their trust and turned their bodies into profiles. Their data into currency. To mirror the biometric economy we already live inside.

03 — Interface
WHAT YOU SEE

A terminal-style web interface styled after financial data dashboards — but trading in heartbeats, stress levels, and confessions instead of currencies.

What if we could see how our intimate data becomes quantifiable and tradeable?

04 — Questions
WHAT IT ASKS

Would you rethink what information you're putting into your phone?

Your personal, intimate information is already living inside a biometric economy. This project just shows you the ledger.

WHAT THIS
PROJECT
ASKS

Both projects sit inside a larger body of work about digital legacy. The AI personas explore how identity fragments and persists through machine interpretation. The biometric stock market reframes bodily data as something that can be quantified, valued, and exchanged.

Together they trace the full arc of how personal data moves in the world — from body, to persona, to commodity. Neither project attempts to define a complete or truthful self. They accept the digital body as partial, contextual, and unresolved.

Technological intimacy is the act of deciding what traces are allowed to remain, circulate, and outlive the individual.
